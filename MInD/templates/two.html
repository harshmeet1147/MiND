Mid-Tier Price Battle: The ₹200-600 price range is the most competitive, with multiple brands capturing similar sales volumes. No single brand dominates, indicating intense competition and brand-switching behavior.

L'Oréal's Stronghold: L'Oréal Paris significantly outperforms others, indicating strong brand equity and consumer preference. Competing brands like Pilgrim, BBLUNT, and Love Beauty & Planet are clustered at a lower but comparable sales level.

Premium Struggles & Niche Plays: Sales drop sharply beyond ₹1,000, suggesting limited traction for premium offerings. However, brands like Nat Habit and Indus Valley are carving out niche segments rather than chasing mass-market volume.

Takeaway: Brands in the mid-tier must differentiate through product innovation or marketing to break through L'Oréal’s dominance. Premium brands need stronger positioning to justify higher price points.
